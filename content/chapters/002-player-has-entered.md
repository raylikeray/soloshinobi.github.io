The first three years of my new life were, in a word, humbling.

I had the mind of an adult trapped in a body that couldn't even hold a spoon properly. Every day was a negotiation between ambition and motor function — and motor function won every single time.

But the System didn't care about my physical limitations.

It appeared on my second day of existence. I was two days old, and the System wanted me to do push-ups.

I stared at the floating blue window from my crib. Shikamaru slept beside me, blissfully unaware of the absurdity of our situation. One twin sleeping peacefully. The other contemplating how to explain to the universe that he literally could not hold up his own head yet.

The penalty was not something I wanted to discover.

It took me three years to complete my first Daily Quest. Three years of slowly, painfully building a body that could do what the System demanded. By the time I managed ten push-ups at age three, most children my age were learning to throw kunai at the Academy prep classes.

But I had something they didn't.

Fourteen Intelligence at level three. The System recognized what I'd brought with me from my previous life — not muscle, not reflexes, but a mind that had already lived once before.

Shikamaru, of course, was probably smarter than me by raw talent. But he also napped six hours a day and called everything "troublesome." The difference between us wasn't intelligence. It was motivation.
